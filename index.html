About

Hi there! I'm Corina.

I’m a UX Researcher + Designer based in Portland, Oregon. I’ve always loved coming up with business ideas and UX has given me a channel for that creative energy. I’m self-driven, committed to the idea that research is a collaborative adventure, and ready to get my hands dirty in a new field. I believe my role in the design process is to function as a gatherer, sensemaker, co-creator and advocate.

I’m currently enrolled in a UX/UI boot to upskill in product development and design skills. I’m eager to contribute to a product team as a researcher or hybrid researcher/designer.

After hours I can be found experimenting in the kitchen or planning my next trip (Puglia, Italy coming right up!)

My core skillset

User Research

I believe research can scale for any project. I use a variety of methods including workshops, 1:1 interviews, surveys, A/B testing, and usability testing to extract actionable insights. I have led discovery workshops with groups of 2-40 people and conducted over 50 1:1 interviews.

Definition & Ideation

Data is only as useful as the story you tell with it. I use affinity diagrams, dot voting, ideation exercises, personas, user journeys to build consensus and generate ideas. My experience in marketing keeps me focused on the business strategy behind the idea.

Information Architecture & Wireframing

Once a team is directionally aligned, I use card sorting, task analysis and analytics to understand users’ intended goals to create simple, fluid information architecture and clear, easy to navigate wireframes — the blueprint for products.
